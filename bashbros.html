Bash Bros (2015)
A 3 vs. 3 Smash Bros. inspired fighting game

Bash Bros is a fighting game all about using a customized team of 3 characters to bash the opposing team off the central platform. Since players don't have control over the character's movement, much of the strategy is about character building and timing. If you want to get an idea of how it works, take a look at the me playing the AI below (I'm the red team).

The project was developed by four Brown University students: Christian Giancarlo, Luke Priebe, Preston Law, and myself. The visual assets were my main contribution to this project. I was responsible for creating the character walking/attack/defend animations, menu buttons/window appearance, all of the backgrounds, and the title screen logo. Other than the alternate skins for characters, the assets were made by me. I also helped program and structure characters and their classes in Java.


About

Basics

The controls are simple: the key shown above each character is that character's attack button. The shaded area that shrinks over time (in the white box with the "S") represents the cooldown time for the character's attack. Once it's all the way down, the character will be ready to attack again.

Character Building

There are 3 stats that can be directly modified: attack (knockback power), defence (resistance to knockback) and speed (walking speed). As each of these are increased, the cooldown time for the attack will increase, meaning that the character has to wait longer before attacking again. Finding a good balance depends on the character's class and its team members in game.

Making a team

A team consists of 3 characters of your choosing. Alhough a lot of time can be put into crafting individual characters, it is equally important to think about how they work on a team. For example, a team of three extremely strong characters might not succeed because of how long they must wait between attacks. Also, team consisting of one class could be rendered useless by a team which can exploit that class's weakness.

Classes

Knight

A knight's best weapon is the sword: it cuts through projectiles from opponent mages, and it can hit multiple opponents if they are close enough. Knights can serve as defensive walls: they are more difficult to knock back because of their heavy armor. This gives them the upper hand when it comes to surviving an attack, but it also ends up weighing them down a bit. This makes them the slowest of the three classes.

Mage

Mages use their powerful magic to attack their opponents from a distance. The projectiles they fire have a bit more power than ordinary weapons, making Mages the most powerful of the classes in terms of attack. There are some downsides, however: the magic can be blocked by an opponent's attack, and the robes they wear aren't enough to keep them safe from attacks, making them prone to attacks.

Rogue

Rogues can't attack opponents directly, rendering them useless when alone. Rogues are, however, known to bee speedy and stealthy, making them the fastest of the three classes. They pefer using traps, which can quickly be tossed down to be activated later. These traps pierce the defences of an opponent, lowering their defences and, consequentially, making them easier to knock out of the arena.
